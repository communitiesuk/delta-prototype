Delta management information (MI) report

Vision and purpose

Why we’re doing this

  • Inspire and engage: show Delta’s impact as critical infrastructure with accessible, insightful, and visually appealing data.
  • Showcase diversity: illustrate the range of Delta’s applications (collections) and engagement across organisations.
  • Enable decision-making: support operational planning with insights that inform decisions on downtime, feature prioritisation, and growth.
  • Answer key questions: provide a snapshot answer to "How’s Delta performing?" and "What’s interesting about Delta right now?"

Report characteristics

  • Ease of access: one-click availability, fully automated with real-time updates.
  • Audience focus: tailored for Delta, FS teams, and SLT, providing a strategic yet concise view.

Core metrics: Delta at a glance

1. Engagement overview

  • Users: total users, breakdown by user type (e.g., admin, read-only).
  • Organisations: number of unique organisations using Delta, by usage type and volume.
  • Sessions: total sessions, average session duration, and usage peaks.

2. Collections and activity

  • Collections overview: count of active, new, and completed collections.
  • Submissions: volume of submissions, averages, and notable changes.
  • Records managed: total records handled, with highlighted additions.

3. Financial metrics

  • Payments overview: total revenue, broken down by collection types if applicable.
  • Expenditures: summary of outflows for collection management or enhancements.
  • Net revenue: high-level summary of financial health and ROI indicators.

4. Collection dynamics

  • Collection timing: insights on collection starts, duration, and extensions.
  • Types and themes: overview of collection types (e.g., funding, compliance) with notable trends.
  • Standout collections: highlights of unique or high-impact collections with relevant data points.

Interactive insights and exploration

1. Smart indexing

  • Quick-access links: dynamic index to collection groups and entity pages for deeper exploration.
  • User access control proposal: address read-only status options for controlled access.

2. Inspirational insight section

  • Interesting trends: snapshot of current trends or unusual patterns to keep the report engaging.
  • Did-you-know facts: eye-catching data points or “fun facts” on Delta’s usage or collections.
  • Monthly highlights: key monthly events showing Delta’s relevance and impact.

3. Future-proofing considerations

  • Adaptability for new collections: considerations to streamline new collection inclusion (e.g., funding service).
  • Blueprint for broader application: aims to create a reporting model for adaptation across other digital products.

Additional goals and design principles

  • Visual and user-friendly design: ensure a polished, engaging look that’s ready for show and tell presentations.
  • Intuitive navigation: user-friendly visuals and data visualisations to clarify complex metrics.
  • Scope clarity: maintain a high-level “bird’s eye view” without diving into detailed analysis.
