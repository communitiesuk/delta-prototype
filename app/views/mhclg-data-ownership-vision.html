MHCLG data ownership vision

Updated: 18 September 2024

A proposal for new MHCLG ways of working with the data assets

The ambition is to have these recommendations adopted and implemented before Christmas 2024.

What is a data asset?

  • Any data collected by MHCLG. Examples: A, B and C
  • Any data built upon data from MHCLG or others. Examples: X, Y and Z

Why are we doing this?

  • Support better ways of working with data - primarily through collaboration
  • A new, simplified, powerful means of working with data
  • Provide a clear understand what data assets exist, how it is being maintained and how it's being used
  • Facilitate more rapid and agile insight provision to the DPM and wider public sector
  • Meet legal obligations regards to data standards

What is the vision (proposed solution)?

Establish for the first time a centalised list of all data assets under the control of MHCLG - the Data Catalogue

The Data Catalog will provide…

  • A one-stop directory of all data assets
  • Details on the data within in each asset, including where possible the data schema
  • Contact details
  • Usage rights and commercial information
  • Links to further information and where the data is held
  • Details on how the data was derived (sources)
  • When the data was last updated and next review date

[Illustration of relationships between Data Asset, Data Catalogue, data source(s)]

How will it be successfully delivered?

The Data Catalogue only works if everyone goes all in; all data catalogued with entries actively maintained.

To achieve this we need to&hellp;

  • New, clear, simple roles with regards to data (built upon CDDO and optimised for MHCLG needs)
  • Monitoring and maintenance requirements backed by tools and workflows that don't add workload burden

Clear roles, simple responsibilities

Primary roles

Responsible for a data asset

Data Guardian

The Data Guardian is responsible for the day-to-day operational activities needed to support the data governance decisions made by SROs in their data domain: Data Management​, Data Quality​, Facilitating Compliance​

Senior Responsible Officer

The Senior Responsible Owner (SRO) has dedicated ownership for a logical grouping of data or data domain and an in-depth knowledge of the overall business strategy in their area.​
