Channel: Grants

Grants lead: Noor Taha - noor.taha@levellingup.gov.uk

DELTA is currently used to support the management of grant claim processing including Neighbourhood Planning Grant and Disabled Facilities grant. Delta allows DLUHC to define structures such as organisations and grants, which makes the system extremely flexible. Delta is not, therefore, limited to specific types of grants and can make payments to any organisation for any grant that has been defined on the system.

Channel: Local Government Finance

Local Government Finance lead: Michael Oliver - michael.oliver@levellingup.gov.uk

The LGF team are responsible for funding for housing and planning as well as separate requests for Local Government and funding around business rates. Payments (including clawback) and billing are managed through Delta. They are also responsible for the central list payments (businesses that transcend more than one local area e.g. railways and BT ).

Data Collections OKR

Objectives and key results
Objective Key results
1. Meet the needs of data collectors and providers
  • 1.1 Local authority users spend less time providing data
  • 1.2 Increased customer satisfaction
  • 1.3 All appropriate collected data to be available in Central Data Store (CDS) automatically and in a timely manner
  • 1.4 Right data provided first time
2. Save time and effort to collect and provide data, and save money
  • 2.1 Reduction in cost per transaction
  • 2.2 Users can complete their task without help and in a quicker time frame
  • 2.3 Complete, consistent, reliable, up to date data
3. Payments
  • 3.1 Payments running correctly
4. Reliability of service
  • 4.1 Unscheduled downtime

UX evaluation questions (aka 'lenses')

When exploring an opportunity for a UX or service enhancement:

  • Which channels does this relate to?
  • Which OKR does this fall under?
  • Which user groups are impacted?
  • Hypothesis: what do we believe the issue (problem) is?
  • How can we validate this? Who do we need to engage with?
  • Qualitative research: what have we seen or heard that provides and a sense of the nature and scale of the issue?
  • Quantitative research: what data exists that provides and a sense of the nature and scale of the issue?
  • How will we prove success? What systems need to be in place to demonstrate improvement?
  • Size (estimate for amount of resource and time to 'fix - small/medium/large)
  • Urgency (low/medium/high)
