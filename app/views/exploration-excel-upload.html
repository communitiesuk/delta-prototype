Improving Excel Upload Experience

Friday, 30 May 2025

Hypothesis

The current method for uploading data via Excel provides such a poor user experience that data providers rarely choose it as their preferred submission method. Instead, they often opt for the more time-consuming, form-based submission process.

Why is the Excel upload experience so poor?

  • The Excel template—especially when auto-generated by Orbeon—is confusing and difficult to use.
  • Inline validation rules within the template are either inconsistent or entirely absent. This allows incorrect data to be entered, which ultimately results in the upload being rejected.
  • There is no meaningful error or validation feedback during the upload process. Users receive no clear indication of what went wrong or how to correct it.
  • In many cases, data providers would prefer to submit via Excel, as they already prepare their data outside Delta in Excel.
  • For more complex data collections, providers find Excel easier to work with because it displays all required fields and guidance in one view. This enables them to input data in any order, without being forced to follow a linear submission path.
  • Excel can be a far more efficient method for submitting multi-record returns, especially when large volumes of data are involved.

Suggested next steps

  • Use the Collections MI Report to analyse:
    • What percentage of collections currently offer Excel as a method of data submission?
    • Of those that do, what percentage of returns are actually submitted via Excel?
  • Conduct heuristic evaluations of all Excel templates—whether auto-generated by Orbeon or manually created by collections managers. Assess their accessibility and usability, and evaluate the clarity of guidance provided, as well as the presence and consistency of inline validation rules.

Reality check

Significantly improving the user experience of submitting data to Delta via Excel upload will only be achievable if:

  • Excel templates can be (and are) designed to be accessible, intuitive, and aligned with Orbeon's validation rules. The validation logic must be fully mirrored in the Excel template to prevent avoidable rejections.
  • The current validation feedback in Orbeon is completely overhauled. A redesigned report must clearly indicate where errors exist and provide actionable guidance for correcting them. However, as the validation functionality resides within Orbeon, it may lie outside the immediate control of the development team.

Stretch goal

Julius and Steve attended a presentation at Service Week 2025 showcasing a flexible approach to importing data via Excel. A key highlight was the system’s ability to accept data in various formats from different users, intelligently identify the structure, and normalise it into a consistent internal format. Exploring a similar approach for Delta could significantly enhance flexibility and adoption.
