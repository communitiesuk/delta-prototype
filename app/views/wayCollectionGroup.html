The English Hedgehog Monitoring Group, part of Defra, plays a crucial role in wildlife conservation efforts across the English countryside. Comprising dedicated experts and researchers, our focus lies in studying and safeguarding the population of hedgehogs, an iconic species deeply ingrained in English culture. Through comprehensive monitoring programmes, we track hedgehog populations, analyse trends, and assess threats to their habitats. Utilising advanced techniques such as GPS tracking and genetic analysis, we gain insights into hedgehog behaviour, distribution, and health, aiding policymakers in formulating effective conservation strategies.

Beyond monitoring, we provide invaluable services to the English government by offering recommendations for habitat preservation and restoration projects. Collaborating with local communities, conservation organisations, and landowners, we implement measures to create hedgehog-friendly environments, including the establishment of wildlife corridors and the installation of hedgehog-friendly fencing. Additionally, we engage in public outreach and education initiatives to raise awareness about the importance of hedgehog conservation and inspire citizen participation in habitat conservation efforts. Through our dedication and expertise, the English Hedgehog Monitoring Group plays a vital role in ensuring the survival of these cherished creatures for generations to come.
